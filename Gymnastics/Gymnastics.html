Gymnastics

Gymnastics has been at every Summer Olympics since 1896. For 32 years, men were only
allowed to compete but in the 1928 Summer Olympics women were allowed to compete in
artistic gymnastics. This sport requires strength, flexibility, agility, coordination, endurance
and control. Rhythmic gymnastics was introduced in 1984 and trampoline events were added in
2000. The nation with the highest number of medals in artistic gymnastics and rhythmic
gymnastics is Russia. In artistic, they have 72 gold, 67 silver and 43 bronze, with a total of
182; in rhythmic, they have 10 gold, 4 silver and 2 bronze, with a total of 16 medals. The
United States is second in artistic gymnastics with a total number 114 medals. The gymnasts
are scored by the difficulty of the elements the gymnast attempts and whether they meet the
composition requirements, landing is also looked at.

Gymnastics has many different events in men and women that require different skills and
strength. In artistic gymnastics some events are...

  • Vault
  • Uneven Bars
  • Balance Beam
  • Floor
  • Pommel Horse
  • Still Rings
  • Parallel Bars
  • Horizontal Bar

Some events in rhythmic gymnastics are...

  • Ball
  • Hoop
  • Ribbon
  • Clubs

Events in trampolining are...

  • Individual Trampoline
  • Synchronized Trampoline
  • Double-mini Trampoline
